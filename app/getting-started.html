Getting Started with #ChicagoScanner

Welcome to the Chicago scanner community! We've prepared these set of steps for you to get started.

Steps to becoming a successful scanner enthusiast

Step 0: Scanner Ethics

As anyone with a scanner knows, what is heard on the air may be confidential or sensitive, either to victims, police/fire, or the general public. Thus, ethics is very important when listening to scanner traffic. Here at CrimeIsDown.com, we have developed our own code of ethics around listening to and reporting information heard on the scanner. You can view our set of rules here.

Step 1: Buy a Scanner

So you want to listen to CPD or CFD dispatches? You'll need a scanner to do this. But not just any scanner - we recommend getting a scanner with P25 digital decoding, as well as trunking capability, if you want to ensure you can hear all the different conversations. CPD uses convential analog channels around 460MHz, while CFD uses conventional digital channels around 477MHz. CPD also has a trunked system (Motorola) for special units.

If you need help choosing a scanner, check out RadioReference's guide.

Step 2: Program the Scanner

Once you have a scanner, you'll need to program it. We recommend using the RadioReference database for Cook County and adding in frequencies for the following (see below). If you own one of the Uniden DMA scanners (e.g. BCD396XT) we will be publishing a set of systems you can download and import.

  • Chicago Police Department - Zones 1-13, Citywide 1-8
  • Chicago Fire Department - Fire Main+Englewood, EMS Main+Englewood, Citywide Fire, EMS Command, Fireground
  • Chicago Transit Authority - All CTA lines, bus supervisors North+South
  • Aviation - Helicopter UNICOM, Fixed-Wing UNICOM
  • Chicago Media - As you see fit from this list.
  • CPD Trunked - If you're up for the challenge, add in the Chicago Public Safety and Services trunked system as well.

Step 3: Start Scanning!

Got your scanner programmed? Time to start scanning! There's a lot you could potentially listen to, but for the most action we recommend you listen to the following:

  • CPD Zones 3, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 12, 13
  • CPD Citywide 1, 2, 6 (10-1 simulcasts go out on all citywides)
  • CFD Fire Main, Fire Englewood, EMS Main, EMS Englewood
  • Helicopter UNICOM
  • CPD Trunked TG352 (Mayor's detail), TG45072 (Marine Unit)

Of course, there may be incidents which take place on a frequency other than these, so check our Social Media Dashboard to see what other folks are listening to.

Step 4: Make Tweaks and Adjustments As Needed

Often you may need to make adjustments to the programming, antenna, or other equipment in order to hear more transmissions or hear them more clearly. We don't have any specific advice on what tweaks to make, but do check out our Scanning 101 page and see if you can find something helpful.
